What this is

A cycling safety score for anywhere in CDMX. You give a start and end; we route a bike trip through brouter and score each 50 m of it against crash density, bike infrastructure, and Ecobici station coverage.

How the score is built

For every ~50 m along your route:
  1. Cyclist-crash risk — KDE of 12,667 cyclist crash points (150 m bw, fatals ×5), divided by citywide P95.
  2. Motor-vehicle risk — KDE of 164,613 all-vehicle crashes, same normalization. Captures road danger where cyclists are rare.
  3. Blend: 0.7 × cyclist + 0.3 × motor.
  4. Infra multiplier — nearest bike lane within 30 m: ×0.50 Ciclovía confinada · ×0.65–0.85 other bike infra · ×1.70 no lane.
  5. Station multiplier — ×1.0 within 300 m of an Ecobici station, ramping to ×1.50 beyond 1.5 km.
  6. Weighted risk = blended risk × infra_mult × station_mult.
  7. Score = 100 × (1 − clip(weighted, 0, 1)).
  8. Caps: no infra + far from stations → max 55. No infra only → max 75. Prevents "no one rides here because it's unrideable" from looking safe.
  9. Low-data warning when < 10 cyclist crashes are within 200 m of the route.
The surface is pre-computed on a 200 m grid at bake time. Your browser does bilinear interpolation along the route — identical math to the Python version, orders of magnitude faster.

Known limits

  • No ridership normalisation — Ecobici doesn't publish OD pairs. Peripheral zones may look safer than they are; the score caps are the current mitigation.
  • brouter's fastbike profile picks the route; we score what it returns, we don't yet prefer safer routes.
  • FGJ data frozen at 2019; SSC dominates 2020–2024.
  • KDE bandwidth (150 m), motor blend (0.3), infra/station multipliers, and score caps are hand-tuned, not learned.
  • Grid quantisation adds ±0.3 points of noise vs. the continuous Python version — indistinguishable in practice.
