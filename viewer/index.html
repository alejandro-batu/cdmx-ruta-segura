What this is

A cycling safety score for anywhere in CDMX. You give a start and end, we route a bike trip through brouter and score it against citywide crash data, fatality-weighted and reduced by any protected bike infrastructure you pass.

Scoring, step by step

For each ~50 m of your route:
  • Cyclist-crash risk — KDE of 12,667 cyclist crash points (150 m bw, fatals ×5), normalized to the P95 citywide.
  • Motor-vehicle risk — same idea, KDE of all-vehicle crashes 2023–2024 (37k points). Blended at 30 %, so dangerous roads where few cyclists ride still raise the score.
  • Infra multiplier — ×0.50 Ciclovía confinada · ×0.65–0.85 other bike infra · ×1.70 no bike lane within 30 m (penalty).
  • Station multiplier — continuous penalty from ×1.0 within 300 m of any Ecobici station up to ×1.50 when >1.5 km away. Proxies "designed for cycling vs. not".
  • Score caps — a point with no bike infra cannot score above 75 near Ecobici or 55 outside the footprint, no matter how low the crash density is. Prevents "no one rides here because it's unrideable" from looking safe.
  • Weighted risk = blended risk × infra_mult × station_mult.
  • Score (0–100) = 100 × (1 − clip(weighted, 0, 1)).
  • Low-data warning surfaces when < 10 cyclist crashes fall in a 200 m buffer around the route.

Known limits

  • No true ridership normalization — Ecobici doesn't publish OD pairs. Station proximity is a crude proxy.
  • brouter's profile picks the route; we don't yet prefer safer routes.
  • FGJ data frozen at 2019; SSC dominates 2020–2024.
  • Motor-vehicle KDE uses 2023–2024 only (~37k points). Earlier years available but not loaded.
  • KDE bandwidth (150 m), blend weight (0.3), and infra/station penalties are hand-tuned.
